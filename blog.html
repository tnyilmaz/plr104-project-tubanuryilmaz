The Cats of Arasta

April 2026

Walking through the Arasta Bazaar, every cat seems to have a story to tell. They sleep on ancient stones and watch the tourists with wise eyes. One particular tabby, whom the locals call 'Sultan', guards the entrance of the old coffeehouse. He doesn't beg for food; he demands respect.

As I sat there sipping my Turkish coffee, Sultan hopped onto the empty chair next to me, purring a melody that felt as old as the cobblestones beneath us. In that moment, I realized these felines are not just animals; they are the true keepers of Safranbolu's history.

Wooden Houses and Ink

March 2026

The architecture of Safranbolu inspires my writing every single day. The wooden frames and white walls hold the secrets of centuries. When the morning mist rolls over the red-tiled roofs, the town feels like a living, breathing novel.

I often write by the window of my restored mansion, listening to the creak of the old timber. It reminds me that every house here has witnessed countless generations. My ink flows easiest when it rains, the rhythmic tapping on the glass blending with the distant echoes of blacksmiths from the canyon below.
